# Twitter Community Guidelines

## Overview

Twitter's mission is to give everyone the power to create and share ideas and information instantly, without barriers. To protect the experience and safety of people who use Twitter, there are some limitations on the type of content and behavior that we allow.

## Authenticity

### Account Identity

- Use your real name and photo to build trust
- Verify your account when eligible
- Don't impersonate others or create fake accounts
- One person, one account (multiple accounts for different purposes is acceptable)

### Content Authenticity

- Share original content or properly attribute sources
- Don't spread misinformation or manipulated media
- Label synthetic or automated content appropriately
- Be transparent about sponsored content

## Safety

### Abusive Behavior

Twitter has zero tolerance for:
- Harassment and bullying
- Hateful conduct
- Violent threats
- Sexual harassment
- Wishful or inciting harm

### Privacy

- Don't share others' private information without consent
- Respect copyright and intellectual property
- Don't doxx or share personal contact details
- Be mindful of location sharing

## Best Practices

### Engagement

1. **Be Respectful**: Treat others how you want to be treated
2. **Stay on Topic**: Keep discussions relevant and constructive
3. **Think Before You Tweet**: Consider the impact of your words
4. **Use Hashtags Wisely**: Help others discover your content appropriately

### Content Quality

- Write clear, concise tweets
- Use images and videos to enhance your message
- Proofread before posting
- Avoid excessive hashtags or mentions
- Don't spam or post repetitive content

### Professional Use

#### For Businesses

- Maintain a consistent brand voice
- Respond to customer inquiries promptly
- Handle complaints professionally and privately when needed
- Disclose sponsored content
- Follow advertising policies

#### For Personal Accounts

- Separate personal and professional content when appropriate
- Be aware your tweets represent you
- Consider your employer's social media policies
- Protect your professional reputation

## Content Restrictions

### Prohibited Content

Twitter prohibits:
- Illegal activities
- Terrorism and violent extremism
- Child sexual exploitation
- Self-harm and suicide promotion
- Graphic violence and adult content (must be marked sensitive)

### Sensitive Media

If posting sensitive content:
- Mark it as sensitive media
- Consider adding content warnings
- Follow age-appropriate content guidelines
- Be aware of cultural sensitivities

## Enforcement

### Violations

Violations may result in:
- Content removal
- Temporary account suspension
- Permanent account suspension
- Legal action in severe cases

### Appeals

If you believe action was taken in error:
- Review the specific violation notice
- Submit an appeal through official channels
- Provide context and evidence
- Be patient during the review process

## Tips for Positive Engagement

### Building Your Community

1. Engage authentically with followers
2. Share valuable and interesting content
3. Support others in your community
4. Participate in relevant conversations
5. Host or join Twitter Spaces for live discussions

### Crisis Management

- Respond quickly to issues
- Take accountability when appropriate
- Move sensitive discussions to private channels
- Know when to step back and let situations cool
- Have a plan for managing negative responses

## Accessibility

### Making Content Accessible

- Add alt text to images
- Use captions for videos
- Avoid excessive emojis that clutter screen readers
- Use clear, simple language
- Provide context for abbreviations

## Conclusion

Following these guidelines helps create a positive, safe, and engaging environment for everyone on Twitter. By being authentic, respectful, and thoughtful in your interactions, you contribute to a healthier social media ecosystem.

Remember: Your digital footprint is permanent. Tweet responsibly.
